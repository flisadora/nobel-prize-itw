  • 1833

    The Beginning

    Alfred Nobel is born in Stockholm, Sweden.

  • 1853-1856

    A Young Man Starts

    After traveling for a few years, Alfred Nobel searches desperately for new products because his father's company goes bankrupt when the Crimean War ends and the Russian military cancel orders. That's when Alfred’s chemistry teacher reminds him of nitroglycerin, and he starts experimenting.

  • 1864

    The First Patents

    Alfred Nobel moves back to Stockholm and obtains the first patent on nitroglycerin (blasting oil) as an industrial explosive, as well as a detonator (blasting cap) for triggering the explosion. Then he moves to Germany to set up the Alfred Nobel & Co Factory in Krümmel near Hamburg.

  • 1866

    Dynamite

    A violent explosion destroys the Krümmel plant in Germay, so Nobel tries to make nitroglycerin safer to handle. He finds that nitroglycerin is stabilized by the addition of kieselguhr, and calls this mixture dynamite. (From greek dynamis meaning ”power”). He also establishes the United States Blasting Oil Company in the US and the Nobel’s Explosives Company in the UK.

  • 1873

    Nobel's Adult Life

    At the age of 40 Alfred Nobel is a wealthy man. He moves to Paris and settles at Avenue Malakoff. Then, his father dies. The manufacture of nitroglycerin and dynamite starts at Ardeer, Scotland, UK.

  • 1876

    Dynamite

    Alfred Nobel advertises for a housekeeper/personal secretary, and hires Bertha Kinsky von Chinic und Tettau. She leaves his employment after a short time and becomes a leading peace activist.

  • 1889

    A Goodbye

    Andriette, Alfred Nobel’s mother, passes away.

  • 1893

    Ragnar Sohlman

    Alfred Nobel hires the young engineer Ragnar Sohlman, who he later names executor of his will and testament.

  • 1896

    The Man Who Never Really Died

    Alfred Nobel passes away, at the age of 63, in his home in San Remo, Italy, on 10 December 1896.

  • 1901

    The Nobel Prize

    The first Nobel Prizes are awarded on 10 December.
